# AAB Research Workflow

AAB research outputs should be transparent, source-traced, and reviewable. This workflow applies to research briefs, working papers, consensus reports, framework crosswalks, and evidence synthesis outputs.

## Stages

1. Scoping: define the research question, audience, intended output, and relation to AAB registry or standards work.
2. Source intake: collect public sources, registry record IDs, dated exports, frameworks, policy signals, or prior AAB outputs.
3. Evidence table: record each source, claim, relevance, limitations, and review status.
4. Draft synthesis: write findings while separating evidence, interpretation, and recommendations.
5. Review: check source trace, uncertainty, public-risk language, developmental fit, and neutrality.
6. Revision: resolve reviewer comments and update evidence tables.
7. Publication decision: route accepted outputs to the website, standards repo, or governance record as appropriate.
8. Maintenance: update or retire outputs when evidence changes.

## Required Elements

- Research question.
- Scope and audience.
- Source inclusion criteria.
- Evidence table or source trace.
- Limitations.
- Review status.
- Publication or reuse recommendation.

## Claim Rules

Every public research claim should trace to at least one of:

- A public source URL.
- A MongoDB/AAB registry record ID.
- A dated public export.
- A standards evidence trace.
- A documented AAB review decision.

## Review Questions

- Is the evidence source public or approved for public citation?
- Does the draft overstate certainty?
- Does it imply endorsement of a provider, tool, program, or school?
- Does it distinguish observed evidence from recommendations?
- Does it identify age band, learner context, or institutional setting when relevant?
